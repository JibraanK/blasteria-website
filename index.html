About Blasteria

Target. Image. Destroy.

With the use of microbubbles and ultrasound imaging, we are able to pinpoint the precise location of harmful bacteria in the body. Once we’ve located the site of infection, we send in High Intensity Focused Ultrasound (HIFU) waves to destroy the bacteria with mechanical force. This way, the bacteria aren't able to mutate and evolve to overcome our treatment.

The Problem

Why AMR?

Globally, antimicrobial resistance (AMR) leads to hundreds of thousands of deaths yearly. AMR is a by-product of misprescribed drugs, accounting for 30% of overall antibiotic usage each year. Someday, resistant microorganisms will mutate faster than the rate at which new drugs are developed. We are in desperate need for an alternative.
